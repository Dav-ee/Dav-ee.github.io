What i have realized so far:

When you are younger, you don’t know where overload level is so much, so you keep taking it on until you find that point. Then you can work out what to reduce so you cope better, but in the process learned the bits that really appeal to you. So as you work those bits out, make sure that when you go to your interviews for another job, you concentrate on the parts that are relevant to that job, but also that you want to learn next. If you have a gap in a particular area, you explain that you have a gap there, but show examples of where you have quickly filled a gap by learning for yourself, asking the right people etc. Demonstrate that if you are not a 100% fit for the job, you will be very shortly after you join the company. And make sure that they are a fit for what you want to do. Ask questions about how they can help you expand your own career development (don’t ask what they are going to give you - ask what opportunities you will have available to grow yourself - they want to know you will guide your own career). Make sure the culture is a good fit for what you want. Do you want a traditional ‘climbing the ladder’ career? Well, good luck. Most companies promote from outside these days to ensure their company doesn’t stagnate.
